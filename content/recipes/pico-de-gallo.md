## Instructions

1. Prepare a large bowl in which all the chopped ingredients will be mixed.
2. Chop the tomatoes into quarters. Optionally, deseed each quarter, which will make the salsa less watery. 
3. Dice the tomatoes into ¼ inch cubes and place them in the bowl.
4. Dice the red onion into the same ¼ inch cubes. Optionally, soak the diced onion in cold water for around 5-10 minutes to dilute the strength of the onion’s pungency. 
5. Mix the onions with the tomatoes.
6. Finely mince the two cloves of garlic and a bunch of cilantro and place in the bowl.
7. Remove the seeds from the jalapeño. Dice into ¼ inch cubes and mix with the other ingredients.
8. Give the limes a quick roll on a flat surface to better release the juices. Cut the limes into halves or wedges, and squeeze the juice into the pico de gallo. 
9. Season the pico de gallo with salt and pepper, olive oil, and optionally, half a packet of chicken bouillon.
10. Mix the pico de gallo until fully incorporated. 
11. Serve alongside tortilla chips*, tacos, or quesadillas, and enjoy!
