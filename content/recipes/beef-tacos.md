## Instructions

1. Start making the cilantro lime sauce by adding cilantro, jalapeños, garlic, mayonnaise, Greek yogurt, lime juice, olive oil, garlic powder, oregano, chicken bouillon, salt, and pepper to a blender.
2. Blend until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed using more salt, lime juice, or sour cream.
4. Refrigerate the cilantro lime sauce until ready to use.
5. To start the taco filling, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. 
6. Add the diced onions to the pan and cook until softened, slight browned, and translucent, around 3-4 minutes.
7. Add the ground beef to the same pan and cook until mostly browned. Add the spice (i.e. garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cumin, oregano, and smoked paprika) and mix until evenly coated.
8. Make a hole in the center of the beef and stir in tomato paste. Cook until caramelized.
9. Add a splash of water to the beef to deglaze the pan, scrape all bits off the bottom of the pan, mix well, and let it simmer with the lid covered until thick and flavourful. Once the water has mostly evaporated, take the pan off of the heat and set aside until assembly.
10. In another pan on medium high heat, add a neutral, high smoke point oil and let it heat up.
11. Once warm, place a tortilla in the pan. Add a generous spoonful of beef to one half of the tortilla and top with a generous amount of shredded mozzarella. 
12. Fold the tortilla over and press gently to seal, making sure to fry the tacos on both sides until golden, crispy, and the cheese is melted.
13. Once finished cooking, drain the tacos on a wire rack or paper towels. 
14. Plate and serve with the cilantro lime sauce, along with any other desired accompaniments*, and enjoy!
