## Instructions

1. Into a food processor or blender, add a roughly chopped serrano pepper or half of a jalapeño pepper.
2. Finely dice the white onion and add 1/3 of the onion to the food processor or blender. 
3. Give one lime a quick roll on a flat surface to better release the juices. Squeeze half a lime’s worth of juice into the food processor or blender, along with a pinch of salt. 
4. Blend until the ingredients form a paste and pour into a separate bowl. 
5. Peel, deseed, and add the avocado flesh, along with cilantro and the rest of the onions. 
6. Mash the avocado to the desired consistency. Mash less for more avocado chunks to give a chunkier texture, and mash more for a smoother, creamier texture.
7. After reaching the desired consistency, squeeze the remaining lime juice into the guacamole. Salt to adjust flavours.
8. Optionally, if choosing to make homemade tortilla chips, cut the corn tortillas into quarters and heat a pot of oil over medium-high heat. 
9. Drop the tortilla quarters into the oil, frying until golden brown and crispy. Drain on a wire rack, seasoning generously with salt. Let cool.
10. Serve the guacamole with the chips on the side, and enjoy! Guacamole can also be enjoyed in burritos, tacos, salads, or with quesadillas.


## Notes

One tortilla makes four chips.
